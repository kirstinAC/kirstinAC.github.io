ArtCenter College of Design proudly hosts our annual Car Classic to showcase the College’s strong ties to the industry and honor many noteworthy alumni. More than just another high-profile car show, this popular public event celebrates the very best in transportation design.

This year, get an up-close-and-personal look at a carefully curated selection of rare and unique coach built classics, hot rods, custom cars and futuristic concepts.

Highlights include:

1934 VOISIN C27 AÉROSPORT
Gabriel Voisin developed automobiles with the mindset of an aeronautical engineer. Many design features follow aircraft construction practice.

1936 STOUT SCARAB
Designed by an aeronautical engineer, its radical streamlined “turtle shell” form anticipated the minivan by several decades. Less than 10 were built.

1957 ALFA ROMEO GIULIETTA SPRINT SPECIALE
Franco Scaglione’s first production design following his 1954–56 Alfa Romeo BAT series, this prototype Giulietta SS debuted at the 1957 Turin Motor Show.
